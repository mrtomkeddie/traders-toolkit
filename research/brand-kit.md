# Tradies Toolbox Brand Kit

This brand kit is based on the current Tradies Toolbox site implementation, especially the palette and typography in `src/index.css` and the messaging and UI patterns in `src/App.tsx`.

## Brand Position

Tradies Toolbox is a modern, no-nonsense web and AI growth partner for trades businesses.

The brand should feel:

- direct
- capable
- modern
- practical
- conversion-focused
- built for real tradespeople, not agencies talking to themselves

## Core Promise

Tradies Toolbox builds websites for the trades and adds AI tools that help them win more work.

Short version:

> Websites built for the trades. AI tools to back them up.

## Audience

Primary audience:

- plumbers
- roofers
- electricians
- HVAC and heating engineers
- other local service-area trades

What they care about:

- getting more enquiries
- looking more professional
- ranking locally
- avoiding calls, meetings, and admin-heavy processes
- owning the finished site outright

## Brand Personality

Use this mix in all materials:

- clear over clever
- confident over hypey
- sharp over corporate
- helpful over salesy
- trades-first over agency-first

The voice should sound like a smart operator who understands how local trade businesses actually buy marketing.

## Messaging Pillars

### 1. Built for the trades

Always make it obvious this is a specialist service, not a generalist agency.

Example phrases:

- Built for plumbers, roofers, electricians and HVAC.
- Trades-only websites.
- Websites designed for local service businesses.

### 2. Async and low-friction

The site leans hard into "no calls required." This is a core brand differentiator.

Example phrases:

- No calls required.
- Fully async.
- Built by message, not meetings.
- No back-and-forth. No wasted time.

### 3. Conversion and local search

The brand promise is not just "nice design." It is performance.

Example phrases:

- Built to rank locally.
- Designed to turn visitors into calls.
- Competitor-researched and conversion-focused.
- Optimised for local search from day one.

### 4. Ownership and simplicity

This is an important trust point.

Example phrases:

- You own the site outright.
- One-off builds, not trap subscriptions.
- No monthly subscription to escape from.

### 5. AI as backup, not gimmick

AI should feel useful and practical, not flashy.

Example phrases:

- AI tools to back your business up.
- AI receptionist for missed-call recovery.
- Practical AI for busy trades businesses.

## Brand Voice Rules

### Write like this

- Use short, punchy sentences.
- Lead with the outcome.
- Use plain English.
- Make the value obvious fast.
- Sound commercially aware.

### Avoid this

- vague agency jargon
- inflated startup language
- "revolutionary" or "game-changing"
- long abstract paragraphs
- sounding too polished or too corporate

## Tone Examples

### Good

- Built to rank in your town and turn visitors into calls.
- No calls, no meetings, no waiting around.
- A bespoke site built to own your local area.

### Avoid

- We deliver cutting-edge digital transformation solutions.
- We empower trade brands through innovative web experiences.

## Taglines

Primary:

> Websites built for the trades. AI tools to back them up.

Alternates:

- Websites for trades businesses that need more calls, not more admin.
- High-converting websites for local trades.
- Trades-only websites, built to win local work.

## Colour Roles

### Orange `#FF6B00`

Use for:

- primary buttons
- key emphasis text
- icons
- badges
- highlights
- active states

Do not use for:

- long body text
- large solid page backgrounds
- overly decorative gradients

### Dark `#0F172A`

Use for:

- headings
- footer background
- dark cards
- nav CTA backgrounds
- premium contrast sections

### Slate `#6B7A8D`

Use for:

- body copy
- secondary text
- captions
- supporting labels

### Light `#F8F7F4`

Use for:

- page background
- soft section backgrounds
- document canvas backgrounds
- premium warm-neutral surfaces

## Typography

The current site uses:

- Display: `Space Grotesk`
- Body: `DM Sans`

### Display font: Space Grotesk

Use for:

- headlines
- section titles
- pricing figures
- strong emphasis moments

Style notes:

- bold
- clean
- modern
- slightly technical without feeling cold

### Body font: DM Sans

Use for:

- paragraphs
- UI labels
- forms
- supporting copy
- proposals and guides

Style notes:

- readable
- tidy
- modern
- friendly without being soft

## Type Hierarchy

Recommended document hierarchy:

- H1: Space Grotesk Bold, 40-56px for web or 28-34pt for docs
- H2: Space Grotesk Bold, 28-36px or 20-24pt
- H3: Space Grotesk Bold, 20-24px or 15-18pt
- Body Large: DM Sans Medium, 18-20px or 12-14pt
- Body: DM Sans Regular, 15-17px or 10.5-12pt
- Small text: DM Sans Medium, 12-14px or 8.5-10pt

## Logo System

Current logo variants in the project:

- `ttlogo.svg`
- `tlogowhite.svg`
- logo assets in `public/Logo (black).svg` and `public/Logo (white).svg`

### Logo usage

- Use black/dark logo on light backgrounds.
- Use white logo on dark backgrounds.
- Keep enough whitespace around the mark.
- Do not recolour the logo beyond approved dark, white, and orange-accented versions.
- Do not stretch, outline, shadow, or place on busy photo backgrounds without a solid backing.

## Visual Direction

The site currently establishes this look:

- warm light background
- dark navy-charcoal anchors
- bright trade-orange accents
- rounded corners
- soft shadows
- clean modern spacing
- subtle grid and glow effects, not loud gradients

This should carry across:

- PDFs
- sales decks
- DNS guides
- onboarding docs
- proposals
- one-pagers
- social graphics

## UI and Document Styling Rules

### Buttons

Primary button:

- orange fill
- white text
- bold label
- rounded-xl shape

Secondary button:

- white fill
- dark text
- subtle gray border

### Cards

- generous padding
- large rounded corners
- soft shadows
- clean borders
- mostly white or dark surfaces

### Section backgrounds

Rotate between:

- white
- brand light
- brand dark

This gives the brand its contrast rhythm.

## Imagery Direction

Use images that feel:

- real
- practical
- trade-specific
- local-business grounded

Preferred imagery:

- real tradespeople
- vans, tools, installs, repair scenes
- laptop/phone mockups showing live sites
- screenshots of finished client websites

Avoid:

- generic agency stock photos
- fake office culture images
- abstract startup art
- overly glossy corporate scenes

## Copywriting Formula

For most assets, use this order:

1. Outcome
2. Who it is for
3. Why it is different
4. What happens next

Example:

> High-converting websites for plumbers, roofers and electricians. Built from competitor research, optimised for local search, and delivered fully async. No calls required.

## Proof and Trust Style

Tradies Toolbox trust signals should feel grounded and believable.

Use:

- specific delivery times
- clear package names
- portfolio examples
- review quotes
- ownership language
- process clarity

Do not rely on:

- empty superlatives
- fake urgency
- inflated claims without proof
